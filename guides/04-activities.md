# Activities: Calls, Meetings & Tasks

**Who this is for:** Every rep who talks to clients and needs the record to remember it — so nothing gets forgotten and anyone picking up an account can see its whole history.

**What you'll learn:** How to log calls and meetings, schedule tasks and follow-ups, and read the activity history on any record.

---

## What counts as an "Activity"?

In Zoho, an **Activity** is any tracked action tied to a record. There are three you'll use daily:

| Activity | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Call** | A phone conversation — logged after the fact, or scheduled ahead |
| **Meeting / Event** | An in-person or video meeting, with a start and end time |
| **Task** | A to-do with a due date — your follow-ups and reminders |

Activities attach to an **Account**, a **Contact**, or a **Deal** — usually more than one at once (a call is with a Contact, about a Deal, at their brokerage Account). That's what builds the **Timeline** on each record.

> **Why bother logging?** The record is the team's memory, not yours. A logged call means the next person — or you in three months — can see what was said without asking. An unlogged call never happened.

## Logging a completed call

Do this right after you hang up, while it's fresh.

1. Open the **Contact** (or **Deal** / **Account**) you spoke with.
2. In the **Activities** / **Open Activities** area, choose **Log a Call** (sometimes under a **+** or **⋯** menu).
   [Screenshot: Log a Call option on a Contact record]
3. Fill in:
   - **Call Type** — Outbound or Inbound
   - **Call Time** — when it happened (defaults to now)
   - **Subject** — a one-line summary, e.g. `Renewal pricing discussion`
   - **Description / Notes** — what was said and agreed (paste a cleaned-up summary — see **[Using ChatGPT for Notes](06-using-chatgpt-for-notes.md)**)
4. Set a **follow-up task** right from the call if there's a next step (see below).
5. **Save.**

## Scheduling a task or follow-up

Tasks are how follow-ups stop slipping through the cracks.

1. On the record, choose **+ New Task** (or **Add Task** in the Open Activities section).
   [Screenshot: New Task button in the Open Activities section]
2. Fill in:
   - **Subject** — the action, e.g. `Send Acme the revised quote`
   - **Due Date** — when it needs to be done
   - **Priority** — High / Normal / Low
   - **Reminder** — set one so Zoho nudges you
   - **Owner** — you, unless you're assigning it to a teammate
3. **Save.** It now shows under **Open Activities** on the record *and* in your own task list / **Activities** tab.

> **The one habit that matters most:** never end an interaction without setting the next task. A call with no follow-up task is a dropped ball waiting to happen.

## Logging a meeting

1. On the record, choose **+ New Meeting** (or **Schedule a Meeting**).
   [Screenshot: New Meeting option on a record]
2. Set the **title**, **date/time**, **participants** (link the contacts attending), and a **location** or video link.
3. **Save.** Upcoming meetings appear under Open Activities; past ones move to the record's history when done.

> If your **[Outlook calendar is synced](07-outlook-email-sync.md)**, meetings can flow between Outlook and Zoho — check with your admin about how calendar sync is set up.

## Reading a record's activity history

- **Open Activities** — what's still to come: scheduled calls, upcoming meetings, open tasks.
- **Closed Activities** — what's already happened: logged calls, completed tasks, past meetings.
- **Timeline** — the full chronological story of the record, activities included.

[Screenshot: Open vs Closed Activities and the Timeline on a Contact record]

This is your pre-call prep in one place: open the contact before you dial and skim what's happened so you're not starting cold.

---

## Staying on top of your day

- **The Activities tab** collects *all* your open tasks, calls, and meetings across every record in one list.
- Build a **[Custom View](05-custom-views.md)** like `My Overdue Tasks` (Due Date is before today) so nothing rots.
- Clear reminders as they pop — an ignored reminder trains you to ignore all of them.

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

| Mistake | Do this instead |
|---|---|
| Logging the call "later" (i.e. never) | Log it right after you hang up |
| A call or meeting with no follow-up task | Always set the next step before you move on |
| Task with no due date | Every task gets a date — that's what makes it actionable |
| Logging to the Contact but not the Deal | Attach the activity to the Deal too, so the opportunity's history is complete |
| Ignoring reminders | Clear or reschedule them; don't let them pile up |

---

## Quick reference

- **Log a call:** open the record → Log a Call → subject + notes → set follow-up task → Save
- **Add a task:** record → + New Task → subject, due date, reminder → Save
- **Add a meeting:** record → + New Meeting → time + participants → Save
- **See what's due:** Activities tab, or a custom view like `My Overdue Tasks`
- **Golden rule:** every interaction ends with the next task set
